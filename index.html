Kyle Anderson

Major League Baseball Player

I officially started my journey of becoming a Major League Baseball player when I was three years old. I found a true love for the sport and vowed from that day forward that I would stop at nothing to make my dream come true. I developed my skills at such a young age simply because I loved it so much that I practiced as much as possible because it was my way of having fun.

When I came to around middle school age I was able to develop my skills even more as I was able to receive mentoring from very well-respected coaches in my area and they built me up as a player and person. Additionally, since I was receiving mentorship from these great coaches I was on teams that had very talented players around me. With my highly competitive spirit I tried my best to outdo my teammates and vice versa. This allowed us all as a collective to get better and hone our skills.

When I finally went to highschool, my biggest jump was made as I got bigger and stronger. I kept my skillset but my body was starting to fill out and I was able to throw harder, build my stamina, and overall get better. It eventually lead me to one of my ulimate goals of playing baseball at a high level at a divison 1 college. There I created new skills I thought I would never achieve and worked as hard as possible to reach my childhood dream. I was blessed enough to be selected in the first round of the draft and completed my long awaited journey.
